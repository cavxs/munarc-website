United Nations Human Rights Council

United Nations Human Rights Council is an inter-governmental body within the United Nations made up of 47 countries whose mission is to promote and protect human rights all around the world. The Human Rights Council focuses on all thematic human rights issues that require attention throughout the year.

Article 18 of the United Nations Human Rights

Everyone has the right to freedom of thought, conscience and religion; this right includes freedom to change his religion or belief, and freedom, either alone or in community with others and in public or private, to manifest his religion or belief in teaching, practice, worship and observance.

“

Based on Article 18, this year's agenda item for the Human Rights Council will be “Promotion and Protection of the Religious Rights of Minorities with a Special Emphasis upon Uyghur Muslims in China”.

The Uyghurs are a mostly Muslim Turkic ethnic group originating from and currently residing in Central and East Asia.

The majority of the Uyghurs live in the Xinjiang area of the People’s Republic of China, where they number about 12 Million people.

Since 2015, China has been detaining Uyghurs in Xinjiang Re-Education Camps. And in 2017, President Xi Jinping issued a directive that "religions in China must be Chinese in orientation" and "adapt themselves to socialist society".

It is now, up to the delegates to protect and secure the religious rights and freedom of the Uyghurs currently residing in China.

Historical United Nations Security Council

The United Nations Security Council is responsible for ensuring the sustainability of peace and security throughout the world. The Security Council contains 15 Member States, 5 of which are considered “Permanent Members” and hold the “Veto” right and the other 10 “Non-Permanent Members” rotating every year.

The UNSC is the only United Nations body, with the authority to issue binding resolutions on its member states and, in rare cases, the Security Council can fall back on forcing sanctions or even approve the utilization of power to keep up or reestablish global harmony and security.

After the attacks, United States president George W. Bush launched the “War on Terror”, an international military campaign targeting several terrorist organisations across the world.
